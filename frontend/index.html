About this map

Overview

Interactive reconstruction of the Mesolithic landscape of Yorkshire, England, at approximately 6200 BCE — roughly 1,000 years before the final flooding of Doggerland.

Data sources

  • Terrain: Copernicus DEM GLO-30 (30 m resolution)
  • River network: OS Open Rivers (modern proxy)
  • Coastline: GEBCO 2023 (−25 m sea-level offset)
  • Lake Flixton: ADS GML data (doi:10.5284/1041580; Palmer et al. 2015)
  • Archaeological sites: ADS — Taylor & Alison 2018

View modes

  • All — terrain + rivers + ecotones + sites + coastline
  • Biotopes — biotope colors + ecotone transitions
  • Terrain — topography + river network
  • Sites only — archaeological sites only

Certainty levels

  • DIRECT Direct archaeological evidence — solid border
  • INDIRECT Indirect / analogical — dashed border
  • INFERENCE Inferred from geomorphology — dotted border
  • SPECULATION Hypothetical reconstruction

Known limitations

  • Terrain classification uses elevation bands — no BGS geology data (planned for v2)
  • River network is modern OS data, used as a proxy for Mesolithic drainage
  • Biotope assignments are knowledge-based estimates from published literature
  • 656 terrain polygons at ~30 m source resolution

Knowledge base: Mezolit2 PoC · Yorkshire bbox W:−2.5° E:0.1° S:53.5° N:54.7°
